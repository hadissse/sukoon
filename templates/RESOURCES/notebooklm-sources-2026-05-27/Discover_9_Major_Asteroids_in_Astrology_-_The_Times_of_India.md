# Discover 9 Major Asteroids in Astrology

TOI Astrology /   Jul 01, 2024, 20:00 ISTShareAA**Text Size**Small

Medium

Large

International Asteroid Day highlights the importance of monitoring asteroids like Ceres for nutrition and care, Pallas Athena for wisdom and intelligence, and Juno for loyalty in partnerships. It also emphasizes asteroids like Eros for love and passion, and Psyche for deep emotional ties.   Image Source: Freepik   International Asteroid day started to spread knowledge about asteroids. It seeks to inform the public on the need of monitoring and comprehending asteroids in order to stop probable future collisions. Let us dive into 9 major asteroids which significantly impact our life in addition to planets Ceres Named for the Roman goddess of agriculture, Ceres stands for nutrition, maternity, and care. Ceres shows on a natal chart our bond with food and the Earth as well as our giving and receiving of care. It reflects our attitude to self-care and our caring manner. A well-known Ceres placement usually denotes a strong need to find fulfillment in helping others and to care for them.  Emphasizing themes of development, sustenance, and mother instincts, this asteroid reminds us of the need of tending to not only ourselves but also those of people around us. Pallas Athena Named for the Greek goddess of wisdom and warfare, Pallas Athena epitomizes intelligence, cunning, and inventiveness. This asteroid stands for human capacity to solve issues, make smart decisions, and produce art. Pallas Athena's arrangement on the natal chart emphasizes our intellectual capacity and our attitude to justice and fairness.  Strong Pallas Athena people might shine in disciplines including law, politics, the arts, and those needing strategic thinking.  It also marks our creative abilities and the manner in which we might apply our brains to negotiate daily obstacles. Juno Named for the Roman goddess of marriage, Juno stands for marriage, loyalty, and alliances. Juno's place in the natal chart in astrology reveals our attitudes toward partnerships and long-term commitments.  It exposes our expectations in a relationship, what we look for in a mate, and how we approach problems of equality and justice inside couples. Emphasizing elements of loyalty, honesty, and partnership harmony, a strong Juno usually denotes a strong want for a devoted and balanced relationship. Vesta  Named for the Roman goddess of the hearth, Vesta stands for purity, loyalty, and clarity. This asteroid speaks to our ability for spiritual devotion and determination.  Vesta's arrangement on the natal chart shows where our inner light resides and how we keep our inner flame burning. Strong Vesta people may be deeply committed to a cause, endeavor, or spiritual journey and have a great sense of direction It emphasizes themes of sanctity and inner focus by pointing out the spheres of our life where we try to keep purity and loyalty. Chiron Named for his wisdom and healing powers despite his own wounds—that of the centaur in Greek mythology—Chiron stands for our own deepest hurts and our capacity for recovery.  The natal chart's placement of Chiron indicates where we hold suffering and how we could turn it into insight and healing for others and ourselves. This asteroid exposes our weaknesses and our path toward personal development and self-awareness.  Chiron reminds us that we might become sources of healing and wisdom for others by first seeing and repairing own wounds. Eros Named for the Greek deity of love and sexual desire, eros stands for attraction, erotic love, and passion.  This asteroid brings to light our romantic inclinations, sexual needs, and passions. Eros's location in the natal chart shows how we show love and passion as well as what sets off our strongest impulses. Emphasizing issues of love, desire, and sensuality, it marks the ways we search and feel pleasure and closeness.  Eros reminds us of the need of connection and enthusiasm in our life. Psyche Named for the Greek goddess whose love tale with Eros reflects the road of the soul, Psyche stands for strong, heartfelt ties and psychological change.  The birth chart placement of this asteroid marks the spheres of life where we want for deep, significant relationships and personal development. Psyche captures our psychological depths and path of self-discovery and metamorphosis. It reminds us of the deeper sides of love and personal development by stressing the need of emotional and spiritual links. Hygiea Named for the Greek goddess of health and cleanliness, Hygiea stands for wellness, hygiene, and health.  This asteroid shows our attitude toward everyday routines, hygiene, and health. Hygiea's position on the natal chart exposes our attitudes toward cleanliness, our bodily and mental health, and our preventative medical practices. Emphasizing issues of wellbeing, purification, and disease prevention, it stresses the need of keeping a neat and healthy lifestyle. Eris Named for the Greek goddess of strife and discord, Eris stands for anarchy, disturbance, and metamorphosis. This asteroid marks spheres of life where we might find conflict, upheaval, and major change. Eris's position in the natal chart exposes our handling of conflict and adversities as well as our capacity for metamorphosis among anarchy. Emphasizing themes of disturbance, conflict, and resiliency, it stresses the need of welcoming change and finding strength in difficulty.
