From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia  

 This is a list of [Aten asteroids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aten_asteroid), a group of [near-Earth objects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Near-Earth_object). As of January 2017[update], there are 1125 known Atens,[ 1 ] most of which remain [provisionally designated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Provisional_designation_in_astronomy) without a number,[ 2 ] as they have not been observed at two or more [oppositions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Opposition_(planets)). The list is divided into [named members](https://en.wikipedia.org#Named_Atens), [brightest](https://en.wikipedia.org#Brightest_Atens), [notable](https://en.wikipedia.org#Selection_of_designated_Atens) and other [record holding](https://en.wikipedia.org#Record-holding_Atens) Aten asteroids. For a list of existing articles, see [Aten asteroids (category)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Category:Aten_asteroids). 

## Named Atens

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=List_of_Aten_asteroids&action=edit&section=1)]  

Aten asteroids are generally named after [Egyptian deities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_deities).[ 3 ] As of February 2024[update], a total of fourteen Aten asteroids have received a name. They are listed in [chronological order](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chronological_order) of discovery.
