ECLIPSES

All based on the nodal axis + lunar cycles There are two eclipse seasons per year, one season near NN + one near SN, with the New + Full Moons (they vary in totality) 

Saros cycles – eclipses repeat in patterns over time, this is great to know for when studying mundane astrology and historical cycles that unfold over time 

They begin at either the North or South Pole and trace a pattern across the Earth There are on average 71 – 73 eclipses in each Saros Series 

Saros cycles: https://eclipse.gsfc.nasa.gov/SEsaros/SEsaros0-180.html 

To see upcoming eclipses: https://www.timeanddate.com/eclipse/ 

You can look at Saros cycle links to decipher meaning that is being carried forth from previous cycles

Prenatal eclipse – similar energetically to the nodal axis, will show what types of destiny themes are present in the life The last solar eclipse to occur before birth 

Great reference book: “Your Prenatal Eclipse” – Rose Lineman 

Use an ephemeris including all  eclipses, locate your birthdate and find the solar eclipse closest in time BEFORE you were born. 

You can also go to astro.com in the extended chart selection to find your prenatal solar eclipse and it will plug it into the chart for you.

PROGRESSIONS

“Progressions are the cosmos in slow motion.”  

Bernadette Brady, 

Predictive Astrology: The Eagle and the Lark

Day for a year formula 30th day after birth will refer to the 30th year of life 

Transits are considered to be more consciously felt and externally projected quickly + since our secondary progressions are based on our infancy and the 90-100 or so days after our birth, they have been ruminating subconsciously for years. Progressions tend to manifest first as feelings, and then if necessary they become external actions. 

Transits are universal (happening to everyone at the same time) while progressions are personal.

For counseling purposes, progressions can be helpful for processing past information, or the stories of our infancies. 

You can read a progression chart in relation to a natal chart in the same way you read a transit. 

To calculate your secondary progressions, find an ephemeris for around the time you were born. Find your birth date, and locate the positions of the planets (your birth chart). Then note the planetary shifts that occur from Day 1 (your birth chart), and you can look day by day to see how your inner life unfolded year by year. 

Pay special attention to when: -       Progressed planets change sign -       Change direction or movement (speed, Rx/direct) -       Form aspects to other progressed planets -       Emphasize a specific repeated degree throughout different planetary placements

Looking at the secondary progressed lunar phases is very helpful to see what you are integrating on a self-concept level. 

Traditional lunar phases: New – 0 – 45˚ ahead of the Sun Crescent – 45 – 90˚ ahead of the Sun 1st quarter – 90 – 135˚ ahead of the Sun Gibbous – 135 – 180˚ ahead of the Sun Full – 180 – 135˚ behind the Sun Disseminating – 135 – 90˚ behind the Sun 3rd quarter – 90 – 45˚ behind the Sun Balsamic – 45 – 0˚ behind the Sun 

Secondary Progressed Moon

When the progressed lunar phase moves within about 12˚ of one of these phase changes, we start to feel it, so in actual predictive technique, we use these degree points to understand more the conscious effects: 

New – 12˚ behind – 33˚ ahead of the Sun Crescent – 33˚ - 88˚ ahead of the Sun 1st quarter – 88˚ – 123˚ ahead of the Sun Gibbous – 123˚ – 178˚ ahead of the Sun Full – 178˚ ahead – 147˚ behind the Sun Disseminating – 147˚ – 102˚ behind the Sun 3rd quarter – 102˚ – 57˚ behind the Sun Balsamic – 57˚ – 12˚ behind the Sun 

Secondary Progressed Moon

Solar arcs are very similar to secondary progressions, but instead of the 1 day for a year method, they progress each planet the same number of degrees as the Sun. 

This allows for all the planets to be used and the interpretations are very similarly founded in solar arc progressions as in secondary progressions.
